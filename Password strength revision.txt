Password strength revision

Composition rules, require a password to have different symbols ( mixed characters) not as beneficial as it would be assumed 
so the use of passphrase which is the use of multiple words was seen as less prone to breaches
Password is not the only reason why breaches could happen. Key factors such as keylogging, phishing, and social engineering also play a key part.

short passwords are easy for brute force attacks to work

password length depends on the threat model be addressed

attacks where the attacker is trying to guess your password can be mitigated, by limiting password attempts

to chances of an attack breaching, depends on how the password is stored, some passwords and a little 'salt', might using an extensive algorithm 
users are encouraged to make their password as long as possible. but to a certain extent as the hashing process requires extensive time

complex passwords are used to increase the difficulty of guessing.
Many services will not allow certain passwords, for example passwords with spaces because it prevents attacks such as the sql injection

and a unhashed passwords would not be sent into the database
such precautions could be seen as very unnecessary 

users tend to use passwords from maybe the dictionary or even worse passwords that have been used to breach for example 'Password1'

For this reason passwords should be compared a blocklist of unacceptable passwords, and should be avoided.

Strong passwords are: 

Long: At least 16 characters long (more is better)
Random: A mix of upper/lowercase letters, numbers and symbols or a passphrase of 5–7 unrelated words
Unique: Used for only one account

For additional security, pair strong passwords with multifactor authentication (MFA)

 password manager makes it easier for employees to follow best practices. It: 

Generates complex passwords
Fills them in automatically
Stores them securely
 use of cloud-based password managers should be avoided and can be risky, especially for security-critical uses
